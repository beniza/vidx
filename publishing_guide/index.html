    📺 VIDX Video Publishing & YouTube Guide

    A Simple Guide to Sharing Your Scripture Videos with the World

    💡 Why Do We Need a Publishing System? (The "WHY")

    Creating high-quality Scripture videos for an entire Gospel or Bible translation is an incredible achievement. However, uploading 28 chapters of Matthew or 260 chapters of the New Testament to YouTube by hand can take days of repetitive typing, copying, and pasting.

    Even worse, YouTube has daily limits on how many videos an automated tool can upload each day (usually around 5 to 6 chapter videos per day per Google project). If an automated tool tries to upload an entire book at once without planning for this, it will fail halfway through and force you to start over!

    How VIDX Solves This: VIDX uses a smart "2-Stage Publishing" system that separates creating your videos from uploading them: 1. Stage 1 (Creation): VIDX generates all your video chapters and writes a small checklist file called an Outbox Manifest (publish_manifest.json). 2. Stage 2 (Uploading): When you are ready, VIDX reads the checklist and begins uploading. When YouTube's daily limit is reached, VIDX cleanly pauses and saves its place. Tomorrow, you simply run the exact same command, and VIDX resumes uploading right where it left off—without re-creating any videos or losing any progress!

    🎯 What You Need (The "WHAT")

    Before you begin publishing, decide which of the two upload methods works best for you or your team members:

    Method Who is it for? What is required?
    🚀 Method 1: Automated Uploading (Recommended) Users who want VIDX to upload directly to YouTube in the background while they work or sleep. • Stable internet connection
    • A one-time Google Cloud setup (free)
    📁 Method 2: Easy Drag-and-Drop Team members with slower internet, or those who prefer uploading manually via their web browser without setting up Google developer credentials. • Just the videos generated by VIDX
    • A web browser open to YouTube Studio

    🛠️ How to Set Up & Publish (The "HOW")

    🚀 Method 1: Automated Uploading (Step-by-Step)

    To let VIDX talk to your YouTube channel automatically, Google requires you to create a free secure "digital key" (called an OAuth Client ID). You only need to do this setup once!

    Step 1: Create your Google Cloud Key (Updated for 2026 Console)

    1. Open your web browser and go to the Google Cloud Console. Sign in with your Google account.
    2. At the top of the page, click Select a project -> New Project. Give it a simple name like VIDX Scripture Uploader and click Create.
    3. In the left search bar or under APIs & Services > Library, search for "YouTube Data API v3" and click Enable.
    4. Go to APIs & Services > OAuth consent screen (in newer Google layouts, this is located under Google Auth Platform / Audience):
    5. Choose External (or Internal if you are inside an official Google Workspace organization) and click Create.
    6. Type an App Name (e.g., VIDX Uploader) and select your email address for developer contact.
    7. ⚠️ CRITICAL STEP — Add Your Team Emails: On the Test users (or Audience) step, click "+ ADD USERS". Type in your own Google/YouTube email address, as well as the emails of any teammates who will be using this key to upload videos. (Because this tool is private for your ministry, Google will block any email address that is not explicitly added to this list!)
    8. Save and finish.
    9. Go to APIs & Services > Credentials:
    10. Click + CREATE CREDENTIALS -> OAuth client ID.
    11. Under Application type, select Desktop app and name it VIDX Desktop. Click Create.
    12. A pop-up window will appear. Click DOWNLOAD JSON to download your key file.

    Step 2: Put Your Key File in Place

    On your computer where you run VIDX: 1. Open your File Explorer and navigate to your Windows home folder (usually C:\Users\YourUsername\). 2. Create a new folder named .vidx (note the period at the start: C:\Users\YourUsername\.vidx). 3. Copy the JSON file you downloaded from Google into this folder and rename it to client_secrets.json.

    Step 3: Launch the Upload!

    Once your video batch has finished generating, open your PowerShell terminal and run:

    vidx --manifest output\matthew_sindhi\publish_manifest.json
    (Tip: Replace output\matthew_sindhi\publish_manifest.json with the actual path to your project's manifest file).

    What happens next: 1. Your web browser will automatically pop up asking you to choose your Google account. 2. Select your whitelisted email address. 3. If Google shows a warning saying "Google hasn't verified this app", don't panic! This is completely normal for private, team-built software. - Click the small Advanced link. - Click "Go to VIDX Uploader (unsafe)" at the bottom. - Check the boxes to allow YouTube access and click Continue / Allow. 4. You will see a success message: "The authentication flow has completed. You may close this window." 5. Check your terminal—VIDX is now securely uploading your chapter videos, applying your title cards as thumbnails, and organizing them into your YouTube playlist!

    💡 Daily Quota Tip: When VIDX uploads 5 or 6 chapters, Google's daily limit will pause the upload. VIDX will safely save its progress. Tomorrow morning, simply run the exact same command again (vidx --manifest ...), and it will instantly resume from where it stopped!

    📁 Method 2: Easy Drag-and-Drop (No Google Setup Needed)

    If you are sharing VIDX with field translators or team members who do not want to set up Google Cloud keys, they can use our built-in "YouTube Studio Ready" offline folders!

    Step 1: Ensure Offline Packages are Enabled

    In your project configuration file (e.g., sindhi_matthew_batch.yaml), make sure publishing is turned off so VIDX doesn't ask for a login, but offline package generation is turned on:

    publishing:
  platform: "youtube"
  enabled: false                 # Set false to generate videos offline without Google login
  generate_offline_package: true # Automatically creates ready-to-use Studio folders!

    Step 2: Run Your Video Generation

    Run your normal batch command: 

    vidx -c examples/sindhi_matthew_batch.yaml

    Step 3: Upload with One Click via YouTube Studio

    When the generation completes, open your project's output folder. You will see a special folder named YouTube_Upload_Package. Inside it, every chapter has its own neat subfolder (e.g., Matthew_Ch01/, Matthew_Ch02/).

    Each chapter folder contains exactly 3 files: 1. The Video (.mp4): Your rendered widescreen or vertical Scripture video. 2. The Thumbnail (title_card.jpg): Your custom branded title card image. 3. The Metadata (metadata.txt): A text file containing the pre-written video title, verse description, copyright text, and hashtags!

    To publish: 1. Open YouTube Studio in your web browser and click Create -> Upload Videos. 2. Drag and drop the .mp4 video file into the browser window. 3. Open metadata.txt and copy-paste the Title and Description directly into YouTube. 4. Under Thumbnail, upload title_card.jpg. 5. Select your playlist and click Publish!

    ❓ Common Questions & Troubleshooting

    Q: Why did my upload fail with "Error 403: access_denied" when logging in?

    Answer: The Google account you are trying to sign in with has not been added to the Test Users list in Google Cloud Console. Go back to Step 1 (OAuth Consent Screen / Audience), click + Add Users, add that exact email address, and try again!

    Q: If my upload drops or my computer restarts, do I have to re-render everything?

    Answer: No! Video generation and uploading are completely separate. Furthermore, VIDX uses Google's resumable chunked upload protocol. If an internet drop disconnects an upload at 75%, running vidx --manifest again will resume uploading from byte 75% without wasting bandwidth or CPU!

    Q: Where can I learn more about the technical design of this system?

    Answer: For software developers and systems architects who want to understand the underlying Python API code, quota management algorithms, and architectural trade-offs, read our technical specification: VIDX YouTube Integration Plan.
